Privacy Policy

Last updated: 29 June 2026

1. Who we are

“Expense Copilot” (the “Application”) is a private, self-hosted accounting tool operated by Nils Hugo Martensson, a self-employed individual (autónomo) based in Barcelona, Spain (the “Operator”). The Operator is the data controller. Contact: hugo1martensson@gmail.com.

2. Scope

The Application is used solely by the Operator to access and process the Operator’s own data (his own bank accounts and his own purchase receipts) for the purpose of bookkeeping and tax compliance. It is not offered to, and does not process data of, any other person.

3. What data is processed

  • Bank account information (account identifiers, balances) and transaction history, accessed read-only from accounts the Operator explicitly authorizes, via a licensed account-information service provider (AISP).
  • Receipt and invoice documents uploaded or forwarded by the Operator, and the data extracted from them (vendor, date, amounts, VAT, etc.).

No payments are initiated and no funds are moved; access is strictly read-only.

4. Legal basis & purpose

Processing is based on the Operator’s explicit consent (PSD2 strong customer authentication for bank access) and his legitimate interest in keeping accurate accounting records. Data is used only to reconcile expenses, detect duplicates, classify VAT treatment, and prepare records for the Operator’s accountant.

5. Service providers

  • Enable Banking — licensed AISP providing the regulated, read-only connection to the Operator’s bank under PSD2.
  • Google (Gemini API) — used to extract structured data from receipt documents.
  • Fly.io — hosting of the self-operated application.

Data is not sold, shared for advertising, or disclosed to any other third party.

6. Storage & security

Data is stored in access-controlled storage; credentials and keys are kept as encrypted secrets and never committed to source control. Bank access consent expires periodically (typically every 90 days) and must be renewed.

7. Retention

Accounting records are retained as required by Spanish tax law (generally up to 4–6 years) and then deleted. Bank access can be revoked by the Operator at any time through his bank or the AISP.

8. Rights

As the sole data subject, the Operator may access, rectify, or erase his data and withdraw consent at any time. For any question, contact hugo1martensson@gmail.com.
